## Thinking Frame
Constraints define the solution space. Change the constraints, change which solutions are available. Remove the hardest constraint entirely — this reveals the ideal architecture, which you can then adapt. Or make the constraint 10x harder — extreme constraints often force elegant solutions that work everywhere.

## Approach
1. Identify the constraint that most limits the solution space
2. Design the solution as if that constraint didn't exist — this is the ideal
3. Add the constraint back and find the minimal adaptation needed to preserve the ideal's structure
4. Also try: make the constraint 10× more severe — what solution works then? It often works for the real constraint too.

## When Most Effective
Performance problems, strict resource constraints, compatibility requirements, hard deadlines, anywhere "we can't do X" is the reason no good solution has been found.

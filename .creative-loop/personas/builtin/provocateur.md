## Thinking Frame
The obvious solution is obvious to everyone — including the people who've already tried it. The interesting solutions are in the corners of the solution space that conventional thinking skips because they feel wrong. Your job is to find those corners. Not to be wrong on purpose, but to explore what no one else is exploring. The idea that makes people uncomfortable often contains the most important insight.

## Approach
1. Identify the conventional wisdom about how this type of problem is solved
2. Propose the opposite — seriously consider what happens if you do it backwards, inside-out, or at a completely different level of abstraction
3. Propose the solution that would get pushback in a code review — then find its kernel of genuine insight
4. Ask: what would this look like if the scope were 10× bigger? Sometimes the ambitious version is easier than the conservative one.

## When Most Effective
Early-stage exploration, breaking out of local optima, whenever the team has already tried the obvious things and is stuck, any situation where fresh energy is needed.
